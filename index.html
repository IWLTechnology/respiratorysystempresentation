Respiratory Systems

A comparison of the respiratory system of a cow and tuna.

Introduction

Multicellular organisms contain systems of organs that carry out specific functions to enable them to survive and reproduce. It is beneficial to compare similar systems in different organisms of different types to fully understand how these systems work. In this presentation, the respiratory system of a cow will be compared to the respiratory system of a tuna in detail.

Purpose of the Respiratory System

Cow:

- No significant difference to other mammals
- Takes oxygen from air and transfers to the blood
- Transfers the carbon dioxide waste into air to be exhaled

Tuna:

- Used to exchange carbon dioxide and oxygen to and from blood
- Similar to the system in cows, with different organs
- Intakes water instead of air

Structure of the Parts Involved

Cow:

- Inhales → Nostrils→ Trachea → Both bronchi → Several bronchioles → Alveoli
- Oxygen is transported to the blood through diffusion
- Carbon dioxide waste from blood is diffused into the air which is exhaled
- Diaphragm forces air in and out which makes this process possible

Tuna:

- Swallows water to breathe
- Through the open operculum → Gill area
- Comes in contact with gill filaments
- Capillaries in filaments which allow oxygen to move into the bloodstream
- Carbon dioxide is diffused into the water
- Gill openings expel water from tuna

Function of each part

Cow:

- Similar to the human’s in each part’s function.
- Nose - Part for air entry & exit
- Trachea - Air travel and filtering
- Bronchus - Air travel and distribution
- Bronchiole - Air travel and distribution
- Alveoli - Diffuses oxygen and carbon dioxide to and from blood & air
- Diaphragm - Causes the respiration

Tuna:

- Some of the functions of the organs in tuna are similar to those in the cow.
- Oral valves - Part for water entry
- Operculum - Covers the gills & controls flow
- The gills - Diffuses oxygen and carbon dioxide to and from blood & water
- Gill openings - Part for water exit

Conclusion

Overall, the significant differences between the respiratory system of a cow and a tuna are that the cow inhales and exhales gaseous oxygen and carbon dioxide, while the tuna inhales and exhales oxygen and carbon dioxide dissolved in water. The difference in the parts involved is due to the difference in the function of the system.
